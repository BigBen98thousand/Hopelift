Our Story

Our journey began in 2011 when our founder, during her NYSC service at Ilesha General Hospital, discovered that true care extends beyond clinical treatment. What started as simple acts of providing toiletries to patients evolved into a lifelong calling.

From dedicating her salary to help orphanage children and widows, to now empowering individuals with business grants and skills training across continents - our mission has always been clear: "Instead of giving people fish, we help them learn to fish."

Our Vision

To create a society where every vulnerable person has equal access to opportunities, support, and sustainable livelihood.

Our Mission

To empower widows, widowers, and the less privileged with financial support, skills training, and emotional assistance for long-term stability and dignity.

Our Programs

Start-Up Business Grants

Financial support for petty trading, small businesses, and income-generating ventures to help beneficiaries become self-sufficient.

Skills Training Program

Practical training in hairdressing, tailoring, baking, catering, farming, and POS operations for sustainable employment.

Widows & Widowers Support

Comprehensive assistance including food packs, emotional support, health checks, and urgent welfare needs.

Financial Literacy & Mentorship

Teaching money management, savings, and business growth strategies for long-term financial stability.

Youth Empowerment Bootcamp

Intensive training for young people with business ideas but no startup funds or guidance.

One-on-One Counseling

Personalized emotional and mental support sessions for individuals facing challenging circumstances.
